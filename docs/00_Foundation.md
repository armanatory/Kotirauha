# Kotirauha

## Overview

Kotirauha is a lightweight multilingual residential documentation platform designed for apartment buildings and shared housing communities.

The purpose of the platform is to help residents document recurring residential disturbances and incidents in a structured, transparent, and neutral way.

The system is not designed to attack, shame, or accuse residents.  
Its purpose is documentation, transparency, and helping housing communities maintain residential peace and habitability.

---

# Problem Statement

Modern apartment buildings are increasingly multilingual and culturally diverse environments.

Residents may experience recurring issues such as:

- Loud noise during quiet hours
- Strong smells in shared corridors
- Smoke or incense spreading into common areas
- Parking violations
- Misuse of shared spaces
- Repeated disturbances affecting sleep or quality of life

In many cases:

- incidents are only discussed verbally,
- evidence becomes fragmented,
- records are inconsistent,
- residents write in different languages,
- and housing boards or landlords receive incomplete information.

As a result:

- recurring disturbances are difficult to document properly,
- communication between residents becomes emotional,
- and building management lacks structured evidence for decision-making.

Kotirauha aims to solve this problem by providing a shared, structured, multilingual incident journal for residential communities.

---

# Goals

The platform aims to:

- Enable residents to document incidents consistently
- Preserve original reports transparently
- Support multilingual communities
- Reduce misunderstandings caused by language barriers
- Improve communication with housing companies and landlords
- Create exportable documentation for official processes
- Encourage factual and neutral reporting

---

# Core Principles

## Neutrality

Kotirauha does not determine guilt or innocence.

The platform only records observations and reported incidents.

---

## Transparency

All records include:

- reporter identity,
- timestamp,
- original language,
- and machine-generated translation information.

---

## Multilingual Accessibility

Residents can write reports in their preferred language.

The system automatically translates entries into the building’s shared language (for example Finnish), while preserving the original wording.

---

## Trust and Integrity

To maintain credibility:

- all entries are timestamped automatically,
- edits are visible,
- deleted records are archived,
- anonymous reporting is disabled.

---

# Target Users

## Residents

Residents can:

- create entries,
- upload optional evidence images,
- browse the shared timeline,
- search and filter incidents.

---

## Housing Community Representatives

Housing company board members or property managers may use exported reports for:

- reviewing recurring disturbances,
- identifying patterns,
- and supporting official warning processes.

---

# Functional Requirements

## Authentication

Users can securely log into the platform.

The platform should support simple, low-friction access suitable for non-technical users.

---

# Incident Entries

Users can create incident reports containing:

- date and time,
- category,
- description,
- optional image,
- apartment number,
- reporter identity.

---

# Incident Categories

The system supports categories such as:

- Noise
- Smell
- Smoking / Incense
- Parking
- Safety Concern
- Common Area Misuse
- Other

---

# AI Translation

## Purpose

The AI translation system enables multilingual participation while maintaining a unified building-wide language for records.

---

## Translation Rules

- The original text is always preserved
- The translated version is clearly marked as machine-generated
- Users can view both versions
- The system never overwrites the original entry

---

# Timeline View

Residents can browse incidents through a chronological timeline.

The timeline supports:

- filtering by category,
- filtering by date,
- keyword search,
- viewing attached images.

---

# Exporting and Reporting

The system can generate exportable reports containing:

- incident history,
- grouped records,
- timestamps,
- reporters,
- and translated versions.

Reports may be used for:

- housing company communication,
- landlord discussions,
- environmental health reporting,
- or internal documentation.

---

# User Experience Goals

Kotirauha should feel:

- calm,
- neutral,
- professional,
- and administrative rather than emotional.

The design language should emphasize clarity, trust, and simplicity.

The platform is not a social network or public complaint board.

It is a residential documentation tool.

---

# Example Workflow

1. A resident notices a disturbance
2. The resident creates an entry in their preferred language
3. The system stores the original text
4. The system generates a Finnish translation
5. Other residents can view the translated entry
6. Entries accumulate into a shared historical timeline
7. Reports can later be exported if needed
